How to choose a toilet?


So, let's begin! A toilet bowl is an item without which it is impossible to imagine a single modern apartment or house. It is difficult to underestimate its role in human life, however, very often insufficient attention is paid to the selection of the most suitable model. We will consider how to choose a toilet bowl, how to choose an installation for a toilet bowl, and also how to choose a toilet bowl correctly without splashing so you don’t regret it later.

The development of plumbing: toilet bowls and the history of their improvement
Today, a toilet is a technical device designed for sanitary purposes, which is equipped with a flush system (automatic or semi-automatic, depending on the model). The existing design today made all hygiene procedures as comfortable as possible, but it didn’t happen right away. The history of the development of toilets dates back several centuries.
Which toilet bowl is best to buy: how to choose a model, depending on the material of manufacture
In total, there are 5 options for materials that can be used for the manufacture of toilets. And each of them has its own advantages and disadvantages, so you should familiarize yourself with them before buying:
  1. Earthenware toilets are extremely popular and in demand, because they differ in low cost. The technology of their manufacture involves firing faience according to a special technology with the application of a special protective coating, which reduces the porosity of the base material. Nevertheless, the pores still cannot be completely closed, so such a toilet becomes much more active than any other, and in order to restore its original appearance, it will take a lot of effort. In addition, such toilets are fragile and often cracks and chips appear on them.
  2. Porcelain toilets are much more durable and reliable products that can function properly for decades. Such toilet bowls also look very attractive, but their cost is quite high. As for the porosity of the material, it is much lower than that of faience, which means it is easier to care for porcelain.
  3. Steel toilets - this is a somewhat non-standard option, however, it has its own advantages. So, for example, it is easy to clean and the material from which it is made is extremely resistant to damage due to mechanical stress. By the way, it is steel products that most often make non-standard shapes. Such toilet bowls also cost a lot.
